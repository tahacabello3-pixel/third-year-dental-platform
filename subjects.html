Dental Medicine & Oral Surgery — complete course overview for Year 3.

Clinical Subjects

Core clinical disciplines you will study and practise in Year 3.

🦷

Oral Medicine

Diagnosis and medical management of oral mucosal diseases, ulcers, white lesions, and orofacial pain. Includes systemic disease manifestations in the oral cavity.

Clinical
4 Credit Hours
🔬

Oral Surgery

Principles of exodontia, surgical extraction of impacted teeth, biopsies, minor oral surgical procedures, haemostasis, and post-operative care.

Clinical
4 Credit Hours
🦮

Prosthodontics

Complete and removable partial dentures, fixed prostheses, crown and bridge work, impression techniques, occlusion, and aesthetic considerations.

Clinical
5 Credit Hours
🔍

Periodontology

Classification and aetiology of periodontal diseases, periodontal examination, non-surgical therapy, scaling and root planing, and surgical treatment planning.

Clinical
3 Credit Hours
⚕️

Conservative Dentistry

Caries detection and management, restorative materials (composites, amalgam, GIC), cavity preparation principles, and endodontic access and instrumentation.

Clinical
5 Credit Hours

Basic Sciences

Supporting biomedical sciences integrated into clinical practice.

💊

Pharmacology

Dental pharmacology covering local anaesthetics, analgesics, anti-inflammatory drugs, antibiotics, antifungals, and drug interactions relevant to dental practice.

Pharmacology
3 Credit Hours
🧬

Pathology

Oral and maxillofacial pathology: benign and malignant lesions, odontogenic cysts and tumours, bone pathology, salivary gland diseases, and histopathological reporting.

Pathology
3 Credit Hours
🧫

Microbiology

Oral microbiome composition, cariogenic and periodontal pathogens, cross-infection control, sterilisation, disinfection protocols, and antibiotic resistance.

Basic Science
2 Credit Hours
